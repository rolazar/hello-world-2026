Welcome to My Page!

My first memory of using a computer was during my first year of middle school. My story really begins when I was eight years old; my mother passed away from pneumonia, leaving my father and me alone. As a single parent, my father felt it was best to leave me in the care of my grandparents so they could look after me while he was busy with work and travel. My family is originally from Kinshasa, the capital of the Democratic Republic of Congo. Growing up, I didn't use computers, perhaps because they were a luxury my parents couldn't afford in the part of the world where we lived.

It wasn't until middle school that I truly entered the world of computing through a school project. My family and I moved to the United States when I was in 5th grade. Upon arriving in a new country, I had to adapt to a different culture and language, which was challenging for a child but also very exciting. Once I mastered the language, it opened my mind and eyes to explore new things. Using a computer opened a whole new world of possibilities for me; it filled a void in my life and created an escape that I found thrilling. Even though I was only learning the basics of computer science in middle school, it felt like learning a new language or exploring unknown territory. It was then that I truly began to explore the possibilities of computing. I fell in love with it, and the rest, as they say, is history.

With Gratitude, Pearla♡
